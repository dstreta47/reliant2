Journey Towards Art

I first began dabbling into 3D back in the year 2009 when I was first introduced to the Open source software Blender. The visceral experience of creating and seeing objects in 3D space left me mesmerised, needless to say, i was hoocked and ever since then I have cultivated 3D art as a serious hobby and a passion of mine. I am adept at creating 3D geometry which includes (but not limited to) Humanoid Characters and creatures, flora, terrain and Interiors for architectural visualization.

My major inspirations for creating 3D art also extends to my love of python scripting and automating processes, In a way, blender was my introduction to the wonderful language that I have come to respect as an indespensible tool of choice; Python. Blender is an opensource software and can be found here , Not only did I learn Blender as a serious hobby, I also took some photography and story-telling studies as a focus to hone my skill for scene composition, colour theory, anatomy, character design and crafting explorable 3D scenes is one of the ways that I see my self getting lost into in the next stages of my 3D journey, with Three.js and Blend4Web becoming a topic of interest for me currently

Recently, I have become become obsessed with portraits and fluids. Fluids and simulation are one of the most intrguing yet difficult thing to master and requires a lot of real world reference study to get right. One of my aims is to simulate some nice simualtions including water-object collision studies, fluids following the usual product visualisation in fancy commercial product

That being said, I've also dabbled a bit into the world of Godot and C# scripting is plenty exciting because Game Development Has long been a passion of mine and something that I have long admired the process of. Game development is both challenging and interesting. Logics break at every turn and yet you are tempted to try and test any solution on the top of your head because of the instant updation and results that can be interacted with.
